What Is LoreAtlas

LoreAtlas is a GPS-powered self-guided walking tour app for Los Angeles. It documents 316 heritage sites across 16 neighborhoods — from 1870 Italianate landmarks to 2022 contemporary bridges. Turn on GPS and move through the city. When you're within range of a documented site, your phone vibrates. Tap to read the hidden history. Log the visit in your field journal. The city becomes a playable map.

This is not a tour guide. There's no group, no schedule, no script. LoreAtlas is for people who want to understand why a building looks the way it does, who died inside it, what was filmed there, and what the original architect intended. It rewards curiosity — whether you're a local who's driven past the same corner for years or a visitor looking for the Los Angeles that exists beyond the Walk of Fame.

A taste of what's out there

316 documented Los Angeles sites. Here are three.

The Bradbury Building
1893 · Downtown LA
A draftsman with no training said a ouija board told him to take the commission. He built the most beautiful interior in Los Angeles. Blade Runner filmed here. So did 500 Days of Summer.
The Cecil Hotel
1924 · Downtown LA
Sixteen deaths. Two serial killers as guests. A water tank mystery that made international news. The most documented haunted site in the American West.
Musso & Frank Grill
1919 · Hollywood
The oldest restaurant in Hollywood. Faulkner and Fitzgerald drank at this bar. The martini is poured tableside from a glass carafe on ice. The waiters remember your name.

Frequently Asked Questions

Questions about LoreAtlas

What is LoreAtlas?
LoreAtlas is a GPS-powered self-guided walking tour app for the hidden history of Los Angeles. It covers 316 documented sites across 16 neighborhoods, including architecture, film locations, haunted places, food institutions, and civil rights landmarks. Your phone alerts you when you're near a place with a story worth knowing.
How does LoreAtlas work?
Turn on GPS and move through the city. When you're within range of a documented site, your phone vibrates and sends a notification. Tap to read the history — a Quick Take for the headline, a Deeper Dive for the full story, and an Editor's Note for the forensic detail nobody else noticed. Log visits in your field journal as you explore.
Is LoreAtlas a self-guided walking tour?
Yes. LoreAtlas is a self-guided GPS walking tour — no group, no guide, no schedule. Walk, drive, or ride through Los Angeles and the app alerts you when there's a story nearby. Explore at your own pace across Hollywood, Downtown LA, Koreatown, Pasadena, Echo Park, Silver Lake, Beverly Hills, Santa Monica, and more.
What kinds of Los Angeles places does LoreAtlas cover?
LoreAtlas covers hidden history, architecture, film locations, haunted sites, music venues, food institutions, civil rights landmarks, Art Deco buildings, industrial heritage, noir history, and celebrity stories across Los Angeles. Sites range from 1870 Italianate landmarks to 2022 contemporary bridges.
Does LoreAtlas track my location?
LoreAtlas uses your phone's GPS to detect when you're near a heritage site, but GPS is optional. Your location is processed locally on your device and never stored as movement history. We log visited places, not your route or movements.
What neighborhoods does LoreAtlas cover?
LoreAtlas covers 16 Los Angeles neighborhoods: Hollywood, Downtown LA, Koreatown, Echo Park and Silver Lake, Pasadena, Beverly Hills, Santa Monica, Culver City, Boyle Heights, West Adams, Fairfax and Mid-City, Ventura, El Segundo, Burbank, North Hollywood, and Venice.
Is LoreAtlas free?
Yes, the core experience is free. Browse all 316 sites with full stories, use GPS proximity alerts, and log visits at no cost. Premium story packs — like Dark Side of LA, Movie Map, and 100 Foods to Eat in LA — unlock deeper content in The Vault, starting at $4.99.
Is LoreAtlas for tourists or locals?
Both. Tourists use LoreAtlas as a self-guided Los Angeles walking tour that goes far beyond the Hollywood Walk of Fame. Locals use it to discover hidden history in their own neighborhoods — buildings they drive past daily without knowing the stories behind them. The app rewards curiosity, not tourism.
🔒

We log visited places, not movement history

Your GPS runs locally on your device. We never store your route, your movements, or your location history. The only thing saved is which sites you've visited. GPS is optional — the Los Angeles walking tour app works fully without it.
